Encouragement of Learning Act

A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
FOR THE ENCOURAGEMENT OF LEARNING, BY SECURING THE COPIES OF MAPS, CHARTS, AND
BOOKS, TO THE AUTHORS AND PROPRIETORS OF SUCH COPIES, DURING THE TIMES THEREIN
MENTIONED.

SECTION 1.
	(1) This Act may be cited as the Copyright Act, as well as the Act for
the Encouragement of Learning.
	(2) This Act may be enforced on persons who are not residents of the
Republic of the Kingdom of Loquntia or any of its protectorates when that
person is a Loquntian citizen or subject.

SECT. 2.
	(1) “MEDIA” is defined under this title as:
		(A) Any map, chart, book or books or any other published
documentary works which are unique and have not been and will not be created
by the imagination of another creator; or
		(B) Any video, audio, hologramme, image or picture, digital
file, television programme, motion picture, design, idea, or any portion
thereof or thereto pertaining which are:
			(i) Unique and have not been and will not be created
by or with the imagination of another creator; and
			(ii) Can be identified, quantified, evidenced, and
documented entirely by the author or proprietor thereof.
	(2) “AUTHOR OR PROPRIETOR” is defined under this title as any person
or similar animal, any corporation, organisation, or any other sentient or
assisted being unless it is a corporation or organisation that has been
dissolved, or has ceased to exist who is the creator of any media as provided
in subsection (1) of this section.

SECT. 3. From and after the passing of this act, the author and authors of
any media already printed within this kingdom, being a citizen or citizens
thereof, or resident within the same, his or their executors, administrators
or assigns, who halt or have not transferred to any other person the copyright
of such media, share or shares thereof; and any other person or persons, being
a citizen or citizens of this kingdom, or residents therein, his or their
executors, administrators or assigns, who halt or have purchased or legally
acquired the copyright of any such media, in order to print, reprint, publish
or vend the same, shall have the sole right and liberty of printing, reprinting,
publishing and vending such media, for as long as that original author shall
live, unless he be not a person, then for a term of thirty years from the
recording the title thereof in the clerk’s office, as is herein after directed:
And that the author and authors of any media already made and composed, and not
printed or published, or that shall hereafter be made and composed, being a
citizen or citizens of this kingdom, or resident therein, and his or their
executors, administrators or assigns, shall have the sole right and liberty of
printing, reprinting, publishing and vending such media, for as long as that
original author shall live, unless he be not a person, then for a term of thirty
years from the time of recording the title thereof in the clerk’s office as aforesaid.

SECT. 4. Further, if any other person or persons, from and after the recording
the title of any media, and publishing the same as aforesaid, and within the
times limited and granted by this act, shall print, reprint, publish, or import,
or cause to be printed, reprinted, published, or imported from any foreign
Kingdom or State, any copy or copies of such media, without the consent of the
author or proprietor thereof, first had and obtained in writing, signed in the
presence of two or more credible witnesses; or knowing the same to be so
printed, reprinted, or imported, shall publish, sell, or expose to sale,
or cause to be published, sold or exposed to sale, any copy of such media,
without such consent first had and obtained in writing as aforesaid, then
such offender or offenders shall forfeit all and every sheet and sheets, being
part of the same, or either of them, to the author or proprietor of such media,
who shall forthwith destroy the same: And every such offender and offenders
shall also forfeit and pay the sum of fifty cents for every sheet which shall
be found in his or their possession, either printed or printing, published,
imported or exposed to sale, contrary to the true intent and meaning of this
act, the one moiety thereof to the author or proprietor of such media, who shall
sue for the same, and the other moiety thereof to and for the use of the
Republic of the Kingdom of Loquntia, to be recovered by action of debt in any
court of record in the Kingdom, wherein the same is cognisable. Provided always,
That such action be commenced within one year after the cause of action shall
arise, and not afterwards.

SECT. 5. Further, no person shall be entitled to the benefit of this act,
in cases where any media, hath or have been already printed and published,
unless he shall first deposit, and in all other cases, unless he shall befor
publication deposit a printed copy of the title of such media, in the clerk’s
office of the district court where the author or proprietor shall reside: And
the clerk of such court is hereby directed and required to record the same
forthwith, in a book to be kept by him for that purpose, in the words following,
(giving a copy thereof to the said author or proprietor, under the seal of
the court, if he shall require the same), ”District of to wit: Be it
remembered, that on the day of [here mark the date] in the year of Our
Sovereign, [here insert the Sovereign’s age], [here insert the author or
proprietor] of the said district, hath deposited in this office the title of
a media, (as the case may be) the right whereof he claims as author or
proprietor, (as the case may be) in the words following to wit: [here insert
the title] in conformity to the act of the Parliament of the Republic of the
Kingdom of Loquntia, entitled ‘An act for the encouragement of learning, by
securing the copies of maps, chart, and book, to the authors and proprietors
of such copies, during the time therein mentioned.’ [here insert the clerk]
clerk of the district of [here insert the district]” For which the said clerk
shall be entitled to receive one half crown from the said author or proprietor,
and one half crown for every copy under seal actually given to such author or
proprietor as aforesaid. And such author or proprietor shall, within two months
from the date thereof cause a copy of the said record to be published in one or
more of the news mediums printed in the Republic of the Kingdom of Loquntia, for
the space of four weeks.

SECT. 6. Further, the author or proprietor of any such media, shall, within
six months after the publishing thereof, deliver, or cause to be delivered to
the Library of Blue Ink a copy of the same, to be preserved, or unto himself
in a media by which it can be proven to have existed by his own creation in a
consistent manner.

SECT. 7. Further, nothing in this act shall be construed to extend to prohibit
the importation or vending, Reprinting or publishing within the Kingdom, of
any media, written, printed, or published by any person not a citizen of the
Kingdom, in foreign parts or places without the jurisdiction of the Republic
of the Kingdom of Loquntia.

SECT. 8. Any person or persons who shall print or publish and manuscript,
without the consent and approbation of the author or proprietor thereof, first
had and obtained as aforesaid, (if such author or proprietor be a citizen of
or resident of the Kingdom) shall be liable to suffer and pay to the said
author or proprietor all damages occasioned by such injury, to be recovered by
a special action on the case founded upon this act, in any court having
cognisance thereof.

SECT. 9. Further, if any person or persons shall be sued or prosecuted for 
any matter, act or thing done under or by virtue of this act, he or they may
plead the general issue, and give the special matter in evidence.

SECT. 10. Reproduction of any works under copyright as aforestated in a manner
which does not incite serious publicity; and is for the purposes of education,
or the exercise of the First Amendment to the Constitution of the Republic of
the Kingdom of Loquntia in a manner which is not intended for pecuniary
benefit shall be protected under that amendment.

SECT. 11. Any person or persons who shall print or publish and manuscript,
without the consent and approbation of the author or proprietor thereof, any
works as herein defined, and claim it as his own creation shall be own in tort
to the creator the full value of the work he violated.

SECT. 12. Any work which has not been filed to truly belong to the proprietor
shall, when a copyright notice is affixed on it, be temporarily secure to him
and the due tort by its reproduction shall continue until its proper filing can
be carried out. Any doubt of the certainty of a copyright on the grounds that
only a copyright notice has been affixed shall grant the benefit of the doubt
the accused of copyright infringement as defined herein.
